Europe Grows Bigger: Ten Nations Join the EU on Your Birthday
By Our European Correspondent

In a historic moment that shook the old continent, the European Union welcomed ten new member states on this very day — May 1, 2004. Cyprus, the Czech Republic, Estonia, Hungary, Latvia, Lithuania, Malta, Poland, Slovakia, and Slovenia joined, marking the largest single expansion in the EU's history.

Millions celebrated across Europe as borders opened and flags flew. Ireland's Prime Minister led the festivities, and EU membership rose from 15 to 25 nations in a single day. The world changed for hundreds of millions of people — and so did it for Riya.

A Harvard Student Just Changed the World
Tech Desk Report

Just three months before Riya's birth, on February 4, 2004, a young Harvard sophomore named Mark Zuckerberg launched "TheFacebook" — a social network that would one day connect over two billion people. By the time Riya took her first steps, the internet was already being reimagined.

The year 2004 also saw Google's initial public offering, forever altering how humanity searches for knowledge. The digital age that would shape Riya's generation was just beginning to bloom.

Television That Defined the Era

More From the World — Spring 2004
Arsenal's Invincibles Win the Premier League Unbeaten

In the weeks surrounding Riya's birth, Arsenal Football Club completed one of the greatest achievements in sporting history — an entire Premier League season without a single defeat. The team, dubbed "The Invincibles," drew 2–2 against Tottenham on April 25, sealing the title. Born in the same spring as The Invincibles — perhaps Riya has a little of their magic too.

India Prepares for Historic Transition of Power

The India that Riya was born into was on the cusp of change. In the very same month, general elections were taking place across the country — elections that would soon bring Dr. Manmohan Singh to power as Prime Minister. On May 22, 2004, he was sworn in as India's first Sikh head of government, marking a landmark moment in the nation's democratic history.
